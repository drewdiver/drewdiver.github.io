Here is a list of the services and applications I run 100% of the time. These are only accessible within a WireGuard mesh VPN.

- [Audiobookshelf](https://audiobookshelf.org)
    - A great audiobook and podcast solution. I managed to get their beta iOS app through TestFlight as well.

- [AdGuard Home](https://hub.docker.com/r/adguard/adguardhome)
    - Nothing against the Pi-Hole team, but this felt much snappier. it also comes with a lot of built in service block lists.

- [Beets](https://beets.io/)
    - As I've stopped streaming, this helps tremendously to tag and organize my music library after importing CD's or miscellaneous digital purchases (Bandcamp etc).

- [FreshRSS](https://www.freshrss.org/)
    - Mostly using the web interface, this has loads of features to help fine tune those pesky sites that are a little more user hostile...

- [IT-Tools](https://it-tools.tech/)
    - A recent addition, if you're like me, I'm not so keen on pasting my text into random websites to perform formatting, validation, etc.

- [Jellyfin](https://jellyfin.org)
    - [Finamp](https://github.com/jmshrv/finamp/releases) on my iPhone as well as ~~[Swiftfin](https://github.com/jellyfin/Swiftfin)~~ [Infuse](https://firecore.com/infuse) for Apple TV and iPhone.

- [Memos](https://www.usememos.com/)

- [Syncthing](https://syncthing.net/)
    - No Google, iCloud etc.. this does exactly as advertised. two-way sync across my devices to my NAS. Most of what I'm syncning is text-based, so [Tarsnap](https://tarsnap.com) is my _offsite_ backup of choice.

- [Librespeed](https://github.com/librespeed/speedtest)
    - _"Free and Open Source Speed Test. No Flash, No Java, No Websocket, No Bullshit._"
